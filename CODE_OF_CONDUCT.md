# Code of Conduct

This project aims to provide a respectful and welcoming environment for everyone who interacts with the repository, whether as a user, contributor, or reviewer.

The principles below apply to all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, documentation, and any related communication channels.

---

## 1. Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- background or identity,
- academic level,
- professional experience,
- technical skill.

---

## 2. Expected Behaviour

Examples of positive behaviour include:

- Being respectful and considerate in all interactions.
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users.
- Using inclusive and precise language.
- Acknowledging and correcting mistakes.

---

## 3. Unacceptable Behaviour

Examples of unacceptable behaviour include:

- Insults, personal attacks, or demeaning comments.
- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind.
- Sharing private information of others without explicit permission.
- Deliberate spam, trolling, or derailment of discussions.
- Any behaviour that would reasonably make others feel unsafe or unwelcome.

---

## 4. Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour:

- You can open a private issue or
- Contact the maintainer directly via the contact details in the repository or dissertation materials.

All reports will be reviewed and handled in good faith. Whenever possible, privacy will be respected.

---

## 5. Enforcement

The project maintainer may take appropriate action, including but not limited to:

- Asking the person to stop the offending behaviour.
- Editing or removing comments, issues, or pull requests.
- Temporarily or permanently restricting project participation.

---

## 6. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and in any context where an individual is representing the project or its community.

If you are unsure whether a behaviour is appropriate, err on the side of respect and professionalism.

---

Thank you for helping to maintain a positive and productive environment.
